Privacy Policy

Effective: 21 June 2026 · Last updated: 21 June 2026 · Data fiduciary: CivicRadar (operated by the project maintainers)

Not an official municipal app. CivicRadar is an independent community tool. It is not affiliated with, endorsed by, or operated by BMC/MCGM, Pune Municipal Corporation (PMC), Thane Municipal Corporation (TMC), or any government body. We deep-link to official corporation channels; we do not submit complaints on your behalf.

CivicRadar (“we”, “us”, “the app”) helps residents of Mumbai, Pune, and Thane report monsoon hazards (such as stagnant water), track municipal complaint progress when they file, and take part in community accountability. This Privacy Policy explains what personal data we collect, why, how long we keep it, and your rights — including under India’s Digital Personal Data Protection Act, 2023 (DPDP Act).

1. Data fiduciary & operator

For purposes of the DPDP Act, the data fiduciary is CivicRadar, operated by the project maintainers. CivicRadar is an independent community project — not an official municipal or government product.

Grievance officer (privacy complaints): Grievance Officer, CivicRadar — configure legal.grievanceEmail before launch. We aim to acknowledge privacy grievances within 7 days and resolve or respond substantively within 30 days, as required under the DPDP Act (subject to applicable rules).

2. Data we collect

  • Display name & ward — chosen during onboarding (stored locally and, if cloud sync is enabled, on our backend).
  • GPS location — latitude/longitude when you explicitly enable location or submit a hazard report. We ask for separate consent before collecting GPS (DPDP). Location metadata embedded in photos is stripped on-device (EXIF removal) before upload.
  • Photos you upload — hazard evidence images. Processed on-device for type/size checks, EXIF stripping, and basic safety screening before storage. Do not upload photos that show other people’s faces without their consent.
  • Report details — hazard type, optional notes, complaint numbers you enter, resolution status, community Me too confirmations.
  • Pledges — optional volunteer supply pledges (type, ward, message).
  • Volunteer signups — ward, neighbourhood/society, hours, skills, and an optional phone/WhatsApp number if you choose to share it with your ward or neighbourhood coordinator. We do not require a phone number for citizens.
  • Device & app data — language preference, coach-mark/onboarding flags, consent flags, and similar settings in localStorage and sessionStorage on your device (see Cookies & local storage below).
  • Anonymous usage analytics — only if you opt in at Terms acceptance. We collect anonymized session events (tab views, report actions, errors, load times). No photos, exact GPS, email, display names, or phone numbers are sent. Ward code only where relevant. Events are stored in the analytics_events table when cloud backend is configured.
  • Anonymous account ID — if cloud sync (Supabase) is enabled, we create an anonymous auth identifier so your reports can sync across devices without a password.
  • Official sign-in (BMC / NGO only) — email address and one-time codes for authorised partner dashboards. Not required for citizens.

We do not require your phone number, Aadhaar, or payment details for citizen features. CivicRadar is free for residents.

3. How we use your data

  • Pin hazards on the community map and show ward-level statistics.
  • Let you track your reports and earn Civic Points for participation.
  • Sync reports across your devices when cloud backend is configured.
  • Enable BMC officials and NGO coordinators (with verified access) to review and resolve reports in their roles.
  • Match volunteer signups to neighbourhood cleanup offers when you opt in.
  • Moderate user-uploaded photos to reduce abuse (client-side checks; server-side review recommended at scale).
  • Improve reliability (offline caching via service worker).
  • Measure anonymous app health — traffic, errors, and performance — only with your analytics consent.

We do not sell your personal data. Future local business sponsorships may show ward-targeted offers in-app; sponsors do not receive your raw GPS, photos, or contact details.

4. Legal basis & DPDP (India)

Under the DPDP Act, we process personal data based on your consent (GPS, photo uploads, optional volunteer contact, optional analytics) and legitimate uses such as compliance with law and responding to grievances. Consent is collected separately where required — Terms acceptance, GPS enablement, and analytics are not bundled into a single mandatory checkbox.

You may withdraw consent for GPS in device settings or via Profile → Withdraw location consent; for analytics via Profile → Withdraw analytics consent; or erase all data via Profile → Delete my data.

As an intermediary under the Information Technology Act, 2000, users are responsible for content they upload. We may remove content that violates our Terms or applicable law.

5. Storage, processors & cross-border transfer

  • On your device — reports, profile, and preferences in browser storage when offline or in local-only mode.
  • Supabase (when configured) — cloud database for reports, pledges, volunteer signups, and analytics. Our backend is hosted in the Mumbai (ap-south-1) region in India, so your data is primarily stored on servers in India. Supabase Inc. is a United States company acting as our data processor. Because project maintainers may administer the service from outside India, some processing involves cross-border transfer under the DPDP Act — we rely on your consent and on our contract with the processor for this. See Supabase Privacy Policy.
  • Third-party CDNs — map tiles (OpenStreetMap/Leaflet), fonts, and optional safety-scan models loaded when online.

Photos are currently stored as compressed data in the database (MVP). Production deployments should migrate to object storage with access controls.

Security note: BMC partner accounts with official *.gov.in email domains may receive elevated dashboard access automatically when signing in — this is role assignment only, not a claim of government endorsement of CivicRadar.

6. Retention

  • Community map reports — retained while relevant to public safety and accountability, typically until resolved and for up to 12 months thereafter for audit/community stats, unless deletion is requested sooner.
  • Pledges & volunteer signups — retained while active; removed when you delete your data or remove your signup.
  • Analytics events — aggregated event rows retained up to 90 days, then purged or aggregated; deleted on request via Delete my data (session-linked rows).
  • Local-only data — remains on your device until you clear app data, withdraw specific consents, or use “Delete my data” in Profile.
  • Anonymous accounts — deleted when you use in-app deletion; residual infrastructure backups may persist up to 30 days.

7. Sharing

Report locations and photos are visible to other app users on the map (community feature). Optional volunteer phone numbers are visible only to NGO coordinators in your ward/neighbourhood scope — not on the public map. If you share to X/WhatsApp, you choose what to share outside the app. We may disclose data if required by Indian law or to protect safety.

8. Children’s privacy & photos of others

CivicRadar is intended for users 18 years and older. We do not knowingly collect data from minors. School or NSS groups should participate only through a parent, guardian, or coordinator who meets the age requirement and accepts Terms on behalf of the group.

Do not upload photos that clearly identify other people (especially minors) without their consent. Hazard photos should focus on the site, not faces.

If you believe a minor has submitted data, contact the grievance officer for removal.

9. Cookies & local storage

CivicRadar is a progressive web app. We use browser localStorage for reports, profile, pledges, volunteer signups, analytics buffer (if consented), and preferences; sessionStorage for anonymous analytics session IDs. We do not use third-party advertising cookies. Clearing site data in your browser removes local copies.

10. Security

We use HTTPS in production, row-level security on the backend, on-device EXIF stripping, and on-device photo checks. No system is perfectly secure; do not upload sensitive documents or selfies with ID cards.

11. Your rights

Under the DPDP Act you may request access, correction, erasure, or withdrawal of consent. In the app:

  • Profile → Delete my data — permanent erasure of your reports, pledges, volunteer signup, confirmations, and session analytics.
  • Profile → Withdraw analytics consent — stops new analytics; clears local analytics buffer.
  • Profile → Withdraw location consent — stops GPS collection until you re-enable.

You may also email the grievance officer. We will respond within the timelines stated in section 1.

12. International users & operator location

The service focuses on Mumbai, Pune, and Thane, Maharashtra, India, and your data is primarily stored in the Mumbai (ap-south-1) region. Project maintainers may administer the service (including support and data requests) from outside India, which means authorised access to your data may occur from outside India. By using CivicRadar you consent to this cross-border access and to processing by our US-based processor as described in section 5. If you access CivicRadar from outside India, you also consent to processing in India.

13. Changes

We may update this policy. Material changes will be reflected in the “Last updated” date. Continued use after changes may require re-acceptance in the app.

14. Contact

Privacy questions and DPDP grievances: Grievance Officer, CivicRadar at configure legal.grievanceEmail before launch, or the support address on your app store listing once published.

Review with qualified Indian counsel before public launch.
